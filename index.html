A.P.J Abdul Kalam




Great Indian scientist and politician who played a leading role in the development of India's missile
and nuclear program


About the legend



  • Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.

  • Kalam graduated in physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and later earned a degree in aerospace engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology

  • He played a pivotal role in India's space program and was a key figure in the development of the Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) at the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).

  • Kalam earned the nickname "Missile Man of India" for his work on the development of ballistic missile technology, particularly the Agni and Prithvi missiles.

  • Dr. Kalam served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007, becoming the first scientist and bachelor to hold this position.

  • Known as the "People's President," Kalam was widely admired for his down-to-earth nature, accessibility, and his ability to connect with people of all ages.

  • He was also an author, having written several books, including his famous autobiography, "Wings of Fire," which chronicles his life and career.
